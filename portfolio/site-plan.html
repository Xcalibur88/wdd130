About Me
Hi! My name is Elijah and I enjoy writing instructions for computers. I started in the industry in 2022 when I got curious about plugin development in the well-known video game Minecraft. What simply started as a way for me to make fun things for friends quickly turned me into a programmer. It turned out there was quite a market on the Minecraft server scene for the full development spectrum including backend systems, networking, databases, websites, and more. Fast forward to today and I am now a first-year Computer Science student with the privilege of working for an e-commerce company based out of Utah. When I’m not studying, my primary focus is on the development and maintenance of our backend ETL processes to deliver consolidated and accurate real-time data to our teams. Outside of work, I enjoy my personal projects and trying new technologies. I love learning and am always excited by the rapid progress we see in the computer space.

Portfolio

What I have done:
Freelance Minecraft Plugin Development As a freelance Minecraft plugin developer, I have worked with numerous clients to create custom plugins tailored to their unique server needs. With a 4.8 average rating on Fiverr, my work has been consistently praised for its quality, efficiency, and reliability.
Public Minecraft Plugin Development, LastLife I developed LastLife, a public spigot plugin that gained significant traction with over 45,000 downloads and a 5-star average rating. Apocalyptic Gaming Network As the Lead Developer for Apocalyptic Gaming Network, I was responsible for designing and maintaining full server systems. My role involved optimizing performance, implementing custom features, and ensuring a seamless experience for players across the network as a whole.
Monolith Brands Group Internship During my time here I learned about large system architectures and each part of the full-stack development process.
